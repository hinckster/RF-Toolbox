BER vs Eb/N0 curves for common modulations. Shannon capacity limit. Required SNR for target BER. Spectral efficiency tradeoff.

BER Formulas (AWGN)
BER_BPSK = Q(√(2·Eb/N0))
BER_QPSK = Q(√(2·Eb/N0)) [same as BPSK]
BER_M-QAM ≈ (4/log₂M)·(1−1/√M)·Q(√(3log₂M·Eb/((M−1)·N0)))
Q(x) = ½·erfc(x/√2). Eb/N0 = SNR/log₂(M) in linear scale.
Shannon Capacity
C = B·log₂(1 + SNR) bits/s/Hz
Eb/N0|_min = (2^(C/B) − 1)·B/C [capacity limit]
Shannon limit: minimum Eb/N0 for reliable comms = −1.59 dB (C/B → 0). BPSK theoretical penalty ≈ +1 dB above Shannon.
Spectral Efficiency
SE = log₂(M) bits/s/Hz [uncoded ideal]
BPSK: 1 · QPSK: 2 · 16-QAM: 4 · 64-QAM: 6 · 256-QAM: 8
Coding rate r reduces SE by factor r. 5G NR: up to 256-QAM + rate 0.93 = 7.5 b/s/Hz per stream.

Modulation & BER — Help
What this tool calculates
Plots theoretical Bit Error Rate (BER) vs Eb/N0 for six digital modulations (BPSK through 256-QAM) over an AWGN channel, alongside the Shannon capacity limit. Uses the exact Q-function approximation (Abramowitz & Stegun, max error 1.5×10⁻⁷) for each modulation order M. Solves the inverse BER equation via bisection to find the Eb/N0 required to hit your target BER.
Controls
  • Eb/N0 (operating): The energy-per-bit to noise-density ratio at your receiver, −5 to 35 dB. Move this to read off which modulations are above or below your target BER.
  • Target BER: The required BER threshold (10⁻³ to 10⁻¹²). Changes the required Eb/N0 column in the results table.
  • Modulation checkboxes: Toggle individual curves on/off. Shannon limit is the theoretical maximum; no real system can cross it.
How to read the charts
BER vs Eb/N0 (left): Log-scale BER on the y-axis; lower is better. Steeper curves reach target BER more quickly but require more Eb/N0. BPSK and QPSK share the same curve. Higher-order QAM curves shift right — they need more Eb/N0 to reach the same BER.

Spectral Efficiency vs Eb/N0 (right): Each dot is a modulation. The purple Shannon bound is the theoretical ceiling. Good modulations cluster near the bound; practical schemes sit 2–5 dB to its right.
Key results
The table shows, per modulation: Eb/N0 req — dBs needed to hit target BER; SE — spectral efficiency in bits/s/Hz; BER @ op — actual BER at your operating Eb/N0. Green/red highlight in the annotation indicates which schemes pass or fail the target at your operating point.
