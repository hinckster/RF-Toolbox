Two-Tone Intermodulation Test

Polynomial device model (a₁, a₂, a₃) → exact IIP3/OIP3/IIP2/P1dB. Spectrum shows IM2, IM3, and harmonics.

Nonlinear Device Model
y(t) = a₁x + a₂x² + a₃x³
x(t) = A·cos(ω₁t) + A·cos(ω₂t)
a₁ = small-signal gain, a₂ → 2nd-order, a₃ → 3rd-order products
Intercept Points (Razavi)
IIP3 = √(4|a₁| / 3|a₃|) [V pk]
IIP2 = |a₁ / a₂| [V pk]
P1dB: 3|a₃|A²/4 = 0.109|a₁|
At IIP3: fundamental power = IM3 power at output (Pozar 10.34)
Output Spectrum Amplitudes
Fund: a₁A + 9a₃A³/4
IM3 (2f₁−f₂): 3a₃A³/4 [close-in]
IM2 (f₁+f₂): a₂A² [wideband]
2nd harm (2f₁): a₂A²/2
A = V peak per tone into 50 Ω

► Things to Try
  • Load the Mixer near compression preset and slowly increase P_in from −5 dBm to +5 dBm — watch the IM3 bars on the spectrum chart grow 3× faster (in dB) than the fundamentals, and the operating point approach P1dB on the intercept diagram.
  • With the LNA preset loaded, set a₂ to 0 and observe that IM2 and all even harmonics disappear — this demonstrates why differential (balanced) amplifiers suppress even-order distortion by cancellation.
  • Make a₃ less negative (e.g., −0.05) while keeping a₁ constant — IIP3 increases dramatically, but P1dB also shifts, illustrating the trade-off between linearity and gain-compression headroom in device biasing.

Two-Tone Intermodulation Test — Help
What this tool calculates
Models a nonlinear device with the polynomial y(t) = a₁x + a₂x² + a₃x³ driven by a two-tone input x(t) = A·cos(ω₁t) + A·cos(ω₂t). From the coefficients it computes exact closed-form intercept points: IIP3 = √(4|a₁|/3|a₃|) V peak, IIP2 = |a₁/a₂| V peak, and input P1dB from the gain-compression condition 3|a₃|A²/4 = 0.109|a₁|. All output spectrum amplitudes (fundamentals, IM2, IM3, harmonics) are computed from the polynomial coefficients at the set input power.
Sliders & inputs
  • a₁ — linear gain (V/V): small-signal voltage gain, 0.1–20 V/V. Gain in dB = 20·log₁₀(a₁). Typical LNA: 5–10 V/V (14–20 dB).
  • a₂ — 2nd-order coefficient (V/V²): drives even-order products (IM2 at f₁+f₂, 2nd harmonics). Zero for a perfectly balanced (differential) circuit. Range 0–10.
  • a₃ — 3rd-order coefficient (V/V³): always negative (compressive nonlinearity). Drives IM3 products at 2f₁−f₂ and 2f₂−f₁ that fall in-band. Range −5 to −0.01; more negative = stronger nonlinearity.
  • Input Power P_in (dBm): power per tone in dBm, −60 to +20 dBm. Converted to V peak via A = √(10^(Pin/10) · 2·50/1000).
  • Center freq f₀ (MHz/GHz): center frequency, 100 MHz to 10 GHz. Affects only the spectrum x-axis labels — intercept calculations are frequency-independent in this model.
  • Tone spacing Δf (MHz): frequency separation between the two tones, 0.1–50 MHz. IM3 products land at f₀ ± 3Δf/2, just outside each tone.
How to read the charts
The Output Spectrum (bar chart) shows output power in dBm for each spectral component sorted by frequency. Blue bars are fundamentals (f₁, f₂); red bars are IM3 (2f₁−f₂, 2f₂−f₁); orange bars are IM2 (f₁+f₂); purple bars are harmonics (2f₁, 2f₂, 3f₁). The Intercept Diagram shows output power vs input power: the blue solid line is the actual compressed fundamental; the red solid line is the actual IM3; the dashed lines are their small-signal asymptotes extrapolated to the IIP3/OIP3 crossover point (yellow diamond). The orange triangle marks P1dB; the green dot is the current operating point.
Key results
  • IIP3 / OIP3: input- and output-referred third-order intercept. IIP3 is the most common figure of merit for mixer and LNA linearity.
  • IIP2: second-order intercept — critical for direct-conversion receivers where even-order distortion creates DC offsets and baseband spurs.
  • P1dB (in): input power at which gain compresses by 1 dB. Roughly IIP3 − 9.6 dB for a pure cubic nonlinearity.
  • IM3 level / IM3 rel.: absolute IM3 power (dBm) and power relative to the fundamental (dBc). At 2 dB above P1dB, IM3 typically reaches −20 dBc.
  • IM2 level: absolute IM2 power (dBm). Relevant in wideband or direct-conversion systems.
