Phase Noise / Leeson's Equation

Oscillator phase noise L(Δf) vs offset frequency. Integrated phase noise, timing jitter, EVM impact.

Leeson's Equation
L(Δf) = 10·log[(2FkT/Ps)·(1+(f₀/2QL·Δf)²)·(1+fc/|Δf|)]
F = noise figure (linear) · k = 1.38×10⁻²³ J/K · T = 290 K · Ps = carrier power · QL = loaded Q · fc = flicker corner
Integrated Phase Noise & Jitter
φ_rms = √(2 · ∫_f1^f2 10^(L(Δf)/10) dΔf) [rad]
t_jitter = φ_rms / (2π · f₀) [s]
Integrate from f1 to f2 (typically 100 Hz to 10 MHz or channel BW)
Three Noise Regions
1/f³: slope −30 dB/dec (flicker dominated, Δf < fc)
1/f²: slope −20 dB/dec (Leeson region, fc < Δf < f₀/2QL)
Flat: thermal noise floor (Δf > f₀/2QL)
Floor = 10·log(2FkT/Ps) · Corner = f₀/2QL

► Things to Try
  • Load the "Sapphire oscillator" preset (Q = 500k): observe the floor drops below −175 dBc/Hz and jitter falls to attosecond levels — the gold standard for metrology.
  • With any preset, increase the flicker corner fc above the Leeson half-bandwidth: the −30 dB/decade region fills the entire useful offset range, dramatically worsening close-in noise.
  • Set integration band from 1 kHz to 10 MHz and compare the TCXO vs VCO presets — the VCO has ~30 dB worse integrated noise, showing why PLLs reference-lock VCOs to clean XOs.

Phase Noise / Leeson's Equation — Help
What this tool calculates
Models oscillator single-sideband phase noise L(Δf) in dBc/Hz using Leeson's equation: L(Δf) = 10·log[(2FkT/Ps)·(1 + (f₀/2QLΔf)²)·(1 + fc/|Δf|)]. The spectrum is plotted from 10 Hz to 100 MHz offset. Integrated phase noise φrms = √(2·∫L(Δf)dΔf) is numerically integrated over the user-defined band, and timing jitter tjitter = φrms / (2π·f₀) is derived.
Sliders & inputs
  • Carrier frequency f₀ (1 MHz–10 GHz, log scale): Oscillator output frequency. Higher f₀ raises the Leeson half-bandwidth f₀/2QL, compressing the 1/f² region.
  • Loaded Q (10–1 M, log scale): Resonator quality factor under operating load. Doubling Q improves close-in phase noise by 6 dB. Crystal oscillators: Q ~ 10k–100k; LC VCOs: Q ~ 10–200.
  • Carrier power Ps (−20 to +20 dBm): Signal power in the resonator. Every 3 dB increase in Ps lowers the thermal floor and 1/f² noise by 3 dB.
  • Noise figure F (0–20 dB): Active device noise figure. Increases the thermal noise floor by F linearly. Typical BJT amplifiers: 1–5 dB; FET oscillators: 3–10 dB.
  • Flicker corner fc (10 Hz–10 MHz, log scale): 1/f noise corner frequency of the active device. Below fc the slope steepens to −30 dB/decade; above it the Leeson 1/f² region dominates.
  • Integration band (From Δf / To Δf): Frequency range over which φrms and jitter are integrated. Typical system choices: 1 kHz–10 MHz for PLLs; 10 Hz–1 MHz for ADC clock jitter.
How to read the chart
The main chart plots L(Δf) in dBc/Hz on a log-frequency x-axis (10 Hz to 100 MHz). Three slope regions are visible: a steep −30 dB/decade section at small offsets (1/f³, flicker-dominated), a −20 dB/decade Leeson region (1/f², thermally noise-upconverted), and a flat thermal noise floor far from the carrier. The purple shaded area marks the user-selected integration band. Region labels beneath the chart color-code the three slopes.
Key results
  • L @ 1 Hz / 10 Hz / 100 Hz / 1 kHz / 10 kHz / 100 kHz / 1 MHz / 10 MHz: Spot phase noise values at all standard offset frequencies, in dBc/Hz. Close-in offsets (1–100 Hz) are typically dominated by the 1/f³ region and can be very high (even positive dBc/Hz).
  • Noise floor: Far-from-carrier thermal limit = 10·log(2FkT/Ps) dBc/Hz.
  • Leeson BW: Half-bandwidth f₀/(2QL) — offsets inside this see the 1/f² rise.
  • Integrated phase noise (φrms): RMS phase error in dBc and radians over the integration band.
  • Timing jitter: Clock jitter in femtoseconds (or attoseconds), critical for ADC SNR and serial data eye diagrams.
