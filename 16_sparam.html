S-Parameter Cascade / Converter

Convert S-parameters to Z, Y, and ABCD matrices. Cascade two 2-port networks. Compute gain, stability factors, and matching metrics.

S → ABCD: A = [(1+S11)(1−S22)+S12S21] / 2S21
Cascade: [T₂] = [T₁]·[T₂], then T → S
T-matrix (ABCD) cascade preserves mismatch interactions between stages
S → Z: Z₁₁ = Z₀[(1+S11)(1−S22)+S12S21] / DẐ
S → Y: Y₁₁ = [(1−S11)(1+S22)+S12S21] / Z₀Dₛ
DẐ = (1−S11)(1−S22)−S12S21    Dₛ = (1+S11)(1+S22)−S12S21
Stability: K = (1−|S11|²−|S22|²+|Δ|²) / (2|S12||S21|)
Δ = S11·S22 − S12·S21   MSG = |S21|/|S12| [dB]
K > 1 and |Δ| < 1: unconditionally stable. K < 1: potentially unstable.

► Things to Try
  • Load LNA + BPF (cascade): compare Block A S21 = 12 dB + Block B S21 = −1 dB. The cascaded result differs from the simple sum (11 dB) due to mismatch interactions at the A–B interface.
  • Set Block A S12 = −60 dB (unilateral LNA): the cascaded S21 collapses to S21_A × S21_B exactly. Raise S12 to −15 dB and watch the error grow.
  • Check the stability factor K for the PA stage preset. Reduce the PA's S22 from −15 to −5 dB and observe how K drops toward 1 — the onset of conditional instability.

S-Parameter Cascade / Converter — Help
What this tool calculates
Accepts S-parameters for one or two 2-port networks (entered as magnitude in dB and phase in degrees at a single frequency) and computes: (1) Equivalent Z, Y, and ABCD (T-matrix) representations. (2) For cascade mode, the combined S-parameters using T-matrix multiplication — [Ttotal] = [TA]·[TB] — which correctly accounts for mismatch reflections at the A–B interface. (3) Stability factors K (Rollett), |Δ|, MSG, and MAG for the active or cascaded network.
Inputs
Each S-parameter is entered as dB magnitude + angle in degrees. Convention: S21 gain of 12 dB → enter 12 in the dB field. Reflection S11 of −15 dB return loss → enter −15. Angles follow the standard microwave convention (negative for phase delay through the device).
Key results
  • K factor: Rollett stability factor. K > 1 and |Δ| < 1 → unconditionally stable. K < 1 → potentially unstable (may oscillate with certain source/load impedances).
  • MSG: Maximum stable gain = |S21/S12| in dB. Upper bound on gain when K < 1.
  • MAG: Maximum available gain (only defined when K > 1). This is the gain achievable with conjugate matching at both ports.
  • Z-matrix: Open-circuit impedance parameters. Useful for series-element modeling and port coupling analysis.
  • Y-matrix: Short-circuit admittance parameters. Natural for parallel-element analysis and FET models.
  • ABCD: Chain (transmission) matrix. A and D are dimensionless transfer ratios; B is in Ω (transimpedance); C is in S (transadmittance). Cascade by matrix multiplication.
Cascade accuracy
Simple multiplication S21_total ≈ S21_A × S21_B only holds when both stages are perfectly matched (|S11|, |S22| ≪ 1). The T-matrix approach used here is exact for arbitrary mismatch and captures the forward/backward reflections between stages.
