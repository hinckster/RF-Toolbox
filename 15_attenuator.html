Attenuator Pad Calculator
What this tool calculates
Computes exact resistor values for π-pad, T-pad (symmetric, equal terminations) and L-pad (impedance-transforming) attenuators. Outputs include exact Ω values, nearest E96 standard resistor, total dissipated power, voltage and current ratings, and a design curve showing component values vs attenuation across the full range.
Topologies
π-pad — Two equal shunt resistors at input/output, one series resistor in the middle. Preferred for RF work due to shunt-to-ground at both ports; easier to implement as a distributed pad.

T-pad — Two equal series resistors with one shunt resistor in the middle. Dual of the π-pad; common in audio and DC-biased circuits where shunt-to-ground at the ports is undesirable.

L-pad — One series resistor (high-Z side) and one shunt resistor (low-Z side) for minimum-loss impedance transformation. Attenuation is inherent, not user-specified — it is the minimum possible loss for that impedance ratio.
E96 nearest values
The E96 series (96 values per decade, 1% tolerance) is the standard for RF and precision work. The tool shows the nearest E96 value and the resulting attenuation error. For critical applications verify the as-built attenuation using a VNA or power meter.
Power ratings
P_in is the total input power. For each resistor the tool distributes the dissipation. The shunt resistors in a π-pad carry less current at high attenuation (they become very large), while the series resistor carries nearly all the input current. Choose resistor power ratings with at least 2× margin.
Things to try
  • Set π-pad, Z₀=50 Ω, A=6 dB — verify R_shunt ≈ 150.5 Ω, R_series ≈ 66.9 Ω (classic result).
  • Increase attenuation to 20 dB and note how R_shunt rises steeply while R_series approaches Z₀.
  • Switch to T-pad at the same attenuation — R_series and R_shunt values are swapped relative to the π-pad.
  • Try the L-pad with Z_s=100 Ω → Z_L=50 Ω: R₁ = R₂ ≈ 70.7 Ω (minimum 7.66 dB insertion loss).
  • Set P_in=1000 mW (1 W) and check the per-resistor power to ensure your chosen packages can handle it.
