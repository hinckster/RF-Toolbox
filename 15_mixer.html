Mixer / Frequency Planning
What this tool does
Computes all difference mixing products f_out = |m·f_RF − n·f_LO| for integer m,n up to the selected max order. Highlights the desired IF (m=n=1), the image frequency (2·f_LO − f_RF), and any products that fall within the IF passband (in-band spurs). The m×n spur grid gives a visual heat-map of which harmonic combinations are problematic for the current frequency plan.
Key concepts
Image frequency — An RF signal at f_img = 2·f_LO − f_RF also produces an output at f_IF after mixing. A pre-selector filter must reject f_img before the mixer.

Half-IF spur — The m=2, n=2 product lands at 2·f_IF, but more importantly f_RF ± f_IF/2 signals produce output near the passband due to the m=2 harmonic. Watch for this in low-IF receivers.

In-band spur — Any product f_out with |f_out − f_IF| < BW is an in-band spur that cannot be removed by the IF filter. These must be addressed by pre-selector filtering, frequency planning, or balanced mixer topologies.

LO Synthesis — Enter a target IF frequency and press "LO low" (f_LO = f_RF − IF) or "LO high" (f_LO = f_RF + IF) to auto-set the LO slider.
Spur grid
Each cell (m, n) is colored by how close |m·f_RF − n·f_LO| is to the desired IF: blue = desired, red = in-band spur, orange = within 10× BW, white = far. Cell (1,1) is always the desired product.
